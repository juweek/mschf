Wassup. My name is Juweek Adolphe and I live in Brooklyn, NY. I'm 24 years old, a big fan of basketball, and the youngest child of two Haitian immigrants.

I'm currently a journalist for the Guardian, but I don't work with words; I draw, I code, and I work with data. I love to play with as many creative mediums as possible, and my dream is to use multiple disciplines to tell meaningful stories, like Mona Chalabi or Giorgia Lupi.

(Move the handle above to see something cool.) Many would say a jack of all trades is a hard sell. I think in this new world of technology, nothing is more fun than challenging convention. Visual arts are finding their ways in places like science labs and newsrooms, and I want to offer a unique perspective - a new and technical one - on what kind of work a visual creative can work on. My personal goal is to merge media together in exciting ways and refine my idea of visual information.

My project proposal is to create a visual newspaper. I will focus on releasing data illustrations that tell stories the current times using dataviz, code, and illustration. This work will be building on the news design experience I already have; past projects include a dataviz on the demographics on Congress, an online calculator that tracks the rise in college costs, and a timeline about the black power movement in America.

Working full-time, I can create one of my data illustrations in about two weeks. I will look up datasets and begin rough sketches in Adobe Draw in the beginning. While I spend some time getting comfortable with the data, I use different Illustrator and Photoshop techniques to refine my ideas and make experiment with visuals. Code comes in pretty dominantly at the end of the process. The end result will be published on an online hub and social media.

I calculated the timeline for this project using my experience in the newsroom today. First, I'll start with the preparation phase, where I'll spend a few weeks researching story ideas and marking important calendar dates of news events to come. After preparation and data collection, I will alternate between working on and releasing my data illustrations, and working on more involved data projects. These more involved projects will have more to do with current events such as the 2020 election. Towards the end of the program, I will spend time collecting my work and thinking about transitioning into the next phase of my career, whether that be a freelance columnist or a venture to keep the publication going.

I will be doing a weekly sketch challenge, posting the sketches on Instagram weekly. I will collect my sketches and also post them on Behance and Twitter. Every month, I will release a work-in-progress post talking about my more involved data projects; these posts will appear on Behance, Instagram, and Twitter and give the community a better understanding of how visual tools serve technical fields like journalism and data science. Finally, I will tweet a lot about my process and hold discussions biweekly. These discussions will be more standard technical advice and mentoring. Possible side projects include youtube tutorials and hosting meetups in NYC.

The budget for this project is straightforward. Most of the costs will go towards traveling costs for conferences and festivals, as I will be actively participating in data viz challenges. I will need some money to make some stories possible, such as covering a political convention. I will need a new macbook, but I have an iPad to draw on, so technology costs are limited. The other costs will be related to publishing the content (website costs, etc).
